Clipplanes are used to visually cut through 3D models within a 3DSpace by hiding geometry on one side of a defined plane. This enables inspection of internal structures without modifying the underlying 3D data, and multiple clipplanes can operate simultaneously.

## Key Capabilities

The feature supports:

- **Interactive positioning**: Defining plane position and orientation in 3D space
- **Dynamic clipping**: Attaching a clipplane relative to the camera for interactive exploration
- **Capping geometry**: Generating surfaces at cut areas to distinguish solid and hollow regions
- **Selective application**: Controlling which nodes in the InstanceGraph are affected
- **Configurable properties**: Adjusting position, normal, distance, transform, and visibility parameters

## Clipping Rooms

A clipping room (clipping volume) consists of six orthogonal planes forming a box. Geometry outside this volume becomes hidden, allowing focused inspection of defined spatial regions. Users can derive clipping rooms interactively from the bounding volume of selected nodes.
