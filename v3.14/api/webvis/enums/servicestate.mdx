The [ServiceState](#) describes the current state of a Service.
The ServiceState is grouped by the ServiceStateCategory which can be discovered by dividing the State by 1000.

```ts
const category = Math.floor(serviceState / 1000);
```

#### See

* ServiceStateCategory
* ServiceType

## Enumeration Members

### CREATED

CREATED: 1000

Indicates that the Service was created.

### ERROR

ERROR: 5000

Indicates a general Error that doesn't match any of the specific ones.

### ERROR\_ABNORMAL\_WS\_CLOSE

ERROR\_ABNORMAL\_WS\_CLOSE: 5002

Indicates that a WS connection closed abnormally.

### ERROR\_NO\_LICENSE

ERROR\_NO\_LICENSE: 5004

Indicates that there was a problem to acquire a license for the Service.

### ERROR\_REQUEST

ERROR\_REQUEST: 5003

Indicates that there was a problem to request the Service from the backend.

### ERROR\_REQUEST\_EXPIRED

ERROR\_REQUEST\_EXPIRED: 5005

Indicates that there was a Timeout while waiting for a response from the Service.

### ERROR\_SHUTDOWN\_TIMEOUT\_REACHED

ERROR\_SHUTDOWN\_TIMEOUT\_REACHED: 5001

Indicates that the Service was not responding for a longer time and the automatic shutdown timeout was reached.

### ERROR\_UNQUEUEABLE

ERROR\_UNQUEUEABLE: 5006

Indicates that the service could not be scheduled or queued.

### READY

READY: 3000

Indicates that the Service is ready to use.

### REQUESTED

REQUESTED: 2000

Indicates that the Service is requested.

### SHUTDOWN

SHUTDOWN: 6000

Indicates that the Service is shutting down

### WARNING

WARNING: 4000

Indicates a general Warning that doesn't match any of the specific ones.

### WARNING\_HTTP\_AND\_WS\_NOT\_RESPONDING

WARNING\_HTTP\_AND\_WS\_NOT\_RESPONDING: 4003

Indicates that the HTTP and WS connection to the Service is not responding.

### WARNING\_HTTP\_NOT\_RESPONDING

WARNING\_HTTP\_NOT\_RESPONDING: 4001

Indicates that the HTTP connection to the Service is not responding.

### WARNING\_WS\_NOT\_RESPONDING

WARNING\_WS\_NOT\_RESPONDING: 4002

Indicates that the WS connection to the Service is not responding.
