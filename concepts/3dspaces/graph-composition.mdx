The InstanceGraph combines **assembly structures** with **sub-assembly structures** from referenced CAD parts into a unified tree.

## Example: Keyboard Key Assembly

A keyboard key assembly (`urn:x-i3d:shape:key`) references two parts:

- `urn:x-i3d:shape:cap` (keycap with geometry)
- `urn:x-i3d:shape:switch` (switch mechanism with geometry)

These form a hierarchical structure where the assembly acts as a container linking the individual parts.

## Scene Graph Aspect

All nodes appear in a **tree structure** where operations propagate downward. Disabling a parent node also disables all child nodes below it in the hierarchy.

## CAD Aspect

Constructions consist of multiple parts, typically corresponding to single CAD resources. An **assembly structure** (defined in files like PLMXML, STEP242, or X3D) references these parts and arranges them. Individual parts may contain their own **sub-assembly** structures.

## Resulting InstanceGraph

The InstanceGraph merges all assembly and sub-assembly structures into one comprehensive tree. Nodes representing parts serve as "transition points" to their corresponding 3D resources. webvis generates a node for every element in this composition, creating the final hierarchical structure that developers and users interact with.
